Frequently
asked questions

Who is this guide for?
Parents, teenagers, young adults, educators, and anyone who wants to understand what vaping actually does — and how to quit. Written in plain language by an ER nurse, not a textbook.
Is this guide really free?
Yes. 100% free. Nurse Charles created this guide because the information needs to get out. Enter your email and the full guide will be delivered to your inbox immediately.
Is vaping really that dangerous?
It's not water vapor. Vape aerosol contains propylene glycol, vegetable glycerin, heavy metals (nickel, lead, chromium), and flavoring chemicals linked to lung disease. When heated, some produce formaldehyde — a known carcinogen.
My kid says they're only vaping nicotine. Should I worry?
Yes. Nicotine is one of the most addictive substances on earth and can establish addiction in adolescents within days. It disrupts brain development in anyone under 25. And many kids don't actually know what's in their cartridges.
What's the connection between vaping and fentanyl?
Fentanyl — 50-100x more potent than morphine — is being found in black-market vape cartridges. A lethal dose is about 2 milligrams, smaller than a few grains of salt. You cannot see, smell, or taste it.
How is today's THC different from what I used?
In the 1970s, marijuana averaged 2-4% THC. Today's vape concentrates contain 60-95% THC — 15-25x more potent. It crosses the blood-brain barrier in seconds. This is not the same substance.
Does the guide include a quit plan?
Yes. Chapter 8 is a complete 8-step quit plan: making the decision, identifying triggers, replacement behaviors, NRT, surviving the first 72 hours, building the first 30 days, support systems, and handling relapse.
